01

Brand Concept

ONYX is a fictional luxury fine jewellery house positioned in the space between heritage craftsmanship and contemporary digital experience. It doesn't try to be Tiffany's or Cartier — it's a darker, more editorial brand. Think the jewellery equivalent of a niche perfume house: opinionated, with a point of view, unapologetically aesthetic.

The brand name itself is the anchor. Onyx is a gemstone — black, lustrous, associated with strength, protection, and mystery. Every design decision should flow from that energy: dark, precise, restrained, quietly confident.

Brand Essence

"We don't make jewellery for every occasion. We make jewellery that becomes the occasion."

Target Audience. Wealthy, design-literate adults — late 20s to 50s — who treat their jewellery as they treat their wardrobe: intentionally. They shop Rick Owens, Bottega Veneta, or Lemaire. They read Apartamento. They're not impressed by logos.

The 3D angle. The WebGL experience isn't a gimmick here — it's the product presentation. Other jewellery sites show flat photography. ONYX lets you be in the room with the piece. That's the entire competitive edge you need to lean into.

02

Voice & Tone

ONYX speaks like a very knowledgeable person who doesn't need to impress you. Short sentences. No exclamation points. No hype. When it does expand, it's poetic — not purple, but precise and sensory. Copy should feel like it was written by someone who has actually held the pieces.

Motion Principles

Slow is luxury. Nothing snaps. Transitions ease in and out over 600–1200ms. The background shader you already have sets the right tempo — everything else should feel like it belongs in that same world.

The 3D ring should not auto-rotate. Replace rotation with idle breathing: a subtle oscillation on the Y-axis (±5°) and a very slow Z drift. The ring should react to cursor position — parallax-driven tilt. This makes it feel alive without being distracting.

Text arrives late. Hero copy fades in after the scene loads, staggered by line. The user should see the ring and background first, then the words. Never the other way around.

Scroll = camera direction. On the homepage, scrolling should feel like moving through space — sections translate upward while 3D elements shift perspective or depth, not just scroll.

R3F Material Setup

Use MeshPhysicalMaterial for all jewellery. Set roughness 0–0.15, metalness 0.9–1.0. Use an HDR environment map (Poly Haven: "studio_small" or "jewelry_store" work well). Add a subtle bloom pass via @react-three/postprocessing. The result will look immediately professional.

07

Interaction Spec

Hero Ring — Idle
Remove auto-rotation. Replace with a slow Y-axis oscillation: rotate between -5° and +5° over 8 seconds, eased with Math.sin(). Add a very subtle Y float (bob ±3px over 6s). This reads as "alive" not "spinning product render."
Hero Ring — Cursor
Map cursor X/Y to ring X/Y rotation offset (±8°). Use a lerp (0.05 factor) so movement is silky, not immediate. This makes the ring feel responsive to presence without being a toy.
Page Enter
Background shader visible immediately. Ring scales from 0.85 to 1.0 over 1200ms (ease out expo). Nav fades in at 800ms. Hero copy lines stagger in at 1000ms, 1200ms, 1400ms. Never show everything at once.
Nav — Hover
Letter spacing expands from 0.3em to 0.45em on hover. Colour transitions white to gold. 300ms ease. The gold bullet separator between links can pulse once on page load.
Collections Grid — Hover
On hovering a product tile: the flat image/render cross-fades to a gentle 3D rotation preview (autoRotate, very slow). The tile lifts 4px, border brightens to gold. Product name and price slide up from below.
Product Page — 3D Viewer
OrbitControls enabled, damping on. Limit polar angle to prevent flipping. Users should be able to rotate freely and pinch-zoom. Add a subtle hint animation on first load: the model slowly rotates 45° then stops — showing that it's interactive.
Scroll — Homepage
Sections use Framer Motion's useScroll + useTransform. As user scrolls past hero, the ring scales down and drifts upward off-screen. The next section (brand statement) rises in from below. This creates a parallax "layer" feeling.
CTA Buttons
No background fill by default — just a 1px gold border and gold text. On hover: border brightens, letter spacing expands by 0.05em, a subtle background fill (5% gold opacity) bleeds in. No scale transforms — that's too playful for this brand.
Custom Cursor
Replace the default cursor with a small circle (16px, 1px gold border, no fill). On hovering anything interactive it expands to 40px and fills with gold at 10% opacity. Implement with a lerped JS position tracker — never snap to cursor position directly.
